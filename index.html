About Me

Eyayu Genet is an Ethiopian artist and lecturer at Bahir Dar University, where he teaches art in the Department of Architecture and has also served in several administrative and committee roles. Born in Durbete, 470 km north of Addis Ababa, Eyayu first earned a bachelor’s degree in economics from Bahir Dar University in 2007. He later pursued his passion for art at Addis Ababa University, Alle School of Fine Arts and Design.

His vivid and expressive paintings draw inspiration from Ethiopian culture, life, joy, and sorrow, while acknowledging God as the source of all things. Using semi-abstract objects, Eyayu often depicts rural scenes, architecture, and cultural elements, giving his works both a modern and deeply traditional essence.

Eyayu’s work has received wide recognition, including both local and international exhibitions, since he started pursuing his passion. His international presence grew further when he was invited to lecture at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas (UNLV), where he spoke on “Bridging History and Modernity in Contemporary Ethiopian Art.”

Through his experimental style, Eyayu invites audiences to reflect on Ethiopian values and the wisdom passed down from generations, continuing to bridge history with modernity in contemporary Ethiopian art.

Lectures & Public Speaking

Eyayu Genet is an experienced lecturer who speaks on the connections between Ethiopian traditional culture and modern artistic development. His engaging talks are often accompanied by exhibitions of his vivid and expressive paintings.
